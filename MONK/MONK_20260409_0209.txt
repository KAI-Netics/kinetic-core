**MONK Mission Authorization Module Technical Description**

The MONK (Modular Operational Network Kernel) Mission Authorization Module implements a cryptographically-secured mission governance framework that validates and enforces autonomous drone operations through a hierarchical trust architecture. The module operates as an embedded tamper-resistant subsystem that processes digitally-signed mission profiles containing geospatial boundaries, kinetic parameters, and temporal constraints, verifying authorization signatures against a distributed certificate authority chain before enabling flight operations. Upon successful validation, MONK generates cryptographic mission tokens that are continuously broadcast via ADS-B and mesh networking protocols to provide real-time identity attestation and operational transparency to airspace authorities and neighboring autonomous systems. The module maintains an immutable mission audit trail through blockchain-based transaction logging, ensuring forensic accountability while implementing fail-safe protocols that automatically terminate operations upon detection of mission parameter violations, communication loss, or cryptographic compromise. This architecture directly addresses critical autonomous systems governance requirements identified by DHS Science & Technology Directorate and DoD autonomous systems frameworks, providing the cryptographic foundation necessary for trusted operation of beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS) drone swarms in contested or cooperative airspace environments.
