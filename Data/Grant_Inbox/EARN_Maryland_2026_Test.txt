EARN MARYLAND GRANT PROGRAM
Fiscal Year 2026 Solicitation
Maryland Department of Labor — Division of Workforce Development

PROGRAM OVERVIEW
EARN Maryland (Employment Advancement Right Now) is a competitive, industry-led workforce training grant program administered by the Maryland Department of Labor. The program funds strategic industry partnerships to train low-wage and entry-level workers for advancement into higher-paying jobs.

FUNDER: Maryland Department of Labor — Division of Workforce Development
PROGRAM: EARN Maryland FY2026
DEADLINE: June 30, 2026
AWARD RANGE: $50,000 to $500,000 per award
ELIGIBILITY: Nonprofit organizations, community colleges, and workforce training providers operating in Maryland that lead a strategic industry partnership with at least two employer partners. Applicants must demonstrate a track record of workforce training delivery.
APPLICATION PORTAL: https://www.dllr.state.md.us/earn/

PRIORITY SECTORS FOR FY2026
- Advanced Manufacturing
- Construction and Infrastructure
- Healthcare and Life Sciences
- Information Technology and Cybersecurity
- Transportation and Logistics (includes emerging technology operators)

FUNDING REQUIREMENTS
- Applicants must be the lead organization of a Strategic Industry Partnership (SIP)
- Minimum two employer partners required at time of application
- Training must lead to an industry-recognized credential or certification
- Programs must serve Maryland residents
- Match funding encouraged but not required for nonprofit applicants
- 501(c)(3) status strongly preferred but not required for initial application; required for award execution

ELIGIBLE TRAINING ACTIVITIES
- Pre-employment training for new workers
- Incumbent worker training for advancement
- Bridge programs for underrepresented populations
- Certification preparation and testing fees
- Equipment and supplies directly related to training delivery

EVALUATION CRITERIA
Applications will be scored on:
1. Quality and completeness of the Strategic Industry Partnership (30 points)
2. Training program design and credential attainment pathway (25 points)
3. Target population served — priority to underrepresented workers (20 points)
4. Employer commitment and job placement plan (15 points)
5. Organizational capacity and past performance (10 points)
